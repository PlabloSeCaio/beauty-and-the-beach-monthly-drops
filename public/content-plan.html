Editorial Note — Strategy & Tone

Outcome-first narrative: July is peak Florida summer — humidity, frizz, brassiness, and vacation prep. Every piece of content answers the question: "Will my hair survive summer?" The month leads with the Summer Hair Reset (keratin/K-18), reinforces trust through named stylist content, and plants seeds for back-to-school.

Persona targeting: Content mapped to Blonde & Color Loyalists, Trust-First New Clients, Healthy Hair Treatment Seekers, Family & Mom Bookers, and Curly & Textured Niche.

Language: Casual, friendly, girl-to-girl. Short natural sentences. No marketing fluff. Use approved message blocks. Avoid: "luxury," "innovative," "seamless," "hassle-free," "game-changing," "revolutionary." Lead with outcomes, not features.

Subject: Keep your blonde cool this summer 💁‍♀️
Preview: Brassiness happens. Here's how to prevent it between visits.

Hi [First Name],

Summer is the hardest season on blonde hair. Pool chlorine, salt water, and UV rays all work together to turn your cool blonde warm and brassy. But a few small habits can make a big difference between appointments.

First: purple shampoo is your best friend — but use it right. Once a week, leave it on for 2-3 minutes. Any longer and it can start to tint your hair violet.

Second: heat protection. Every time you use a hot tool, you're opening the cuticle and letting brassiness creep in. A Davines heat protectant keeps your color safe and your hair healthy.

Third: refresh your toner. If you're seeing brassiness, don't wait 4 more weeks. A quick gloss treatment can reset your tone and buy you time until your full appointment.

Keratin vs. K-18: Which One Does Your Hair Need?
Slide Copy
Slide 1: Keratin and K-18 both help damaged hair — but they do different things. Here's how to choose.
