5 Questions to Ask Before Your First Salon Visit

Walking into a new salon for the first time can feel like a blind date. You're hopeful, but you've been burned before. Here are five questions to ask — and what great answers sound like.

1. "Will You Actually Listen to What I Want?"

This is the number one fear. You've sat in a chair before, told a stylist what you wanted, and walked out with something completely different. It's not just disappointing — it's expensive.

At Beauty and the Beach Salon, every service starts with a real consultation. You sit down with your stylist and talk about your hair history, your routine, and what you actually want. Not what they think you should want. Your stylist will ask questions, look at your hair, and confirm the plan before touching a single strand.

Our clients tell us the same: our stylists listen, take the consultation seriously, and deliver results they love.

2. "How Much Will It Cost — Really?"

Surprise charges are a top complaint in the salon world. You should never leave wondering why the price was higher than what you discussed.

Here's how we handle it: we confirm the price before we start. If anything changes during the service — if we need extra product or time — we ask for your approval first. No hidden fees, no "extra tint" without asking.

You can also browse our full service menu online to see pricing before you book.

3. "What If I Don't Like It?"

Even with the best consultation, things can go wrong. What matters is how the salon handles it.

At Beauty and the Beach Salon, we check in during the service — not just at the end. Your stylist will ask how things feel and if any adjustments are needed. After your appointment, we send a survey text to hear about your experience.

If something isn't right, tell us. We'll fix it.

4. "Which Stylist Should I Book With?"

This is the secret to a great salon experience: book the right person for your hair. Not every stylist is the best fit for every service, and that's okay.

At Beauty and the Beach Salon, each stylist has their own specialty — from precision cuts and dimensional color to blonding and keratin treatments. Our team page walks you through everyone's expertise so you can choose the right fit for your hair.

Our clients don't just book the salon — they book by name, because they trust the person. Meet our full team here.

5. "How Do I Book?"

Booking should be the easiest part. You can book online 24/7 in about 60 seconds. No phone tag, no waiting for a call back.

If you prefer, you can call the salon during business hours. And if you're not sure what service you need, just book a consultation — we'll figure it out together.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to know exactly what I want before I come in?

Not at all. That's what the consultation is for. If you're not sure, tell your stylist. They'll help you figure out what will work best for your hair, your face shape, and your lifestyle.

How long does a first visit usually take?

Plan for about 60–90 minutes for a cut, and 2–3 hours for color. Your stylist will give you a time estimate during the consultation.

Can I bring photos of what I want?

Yes — please do! Reference photos help your stylist understand exactly what you're looking for. Just remember that your hair texture and face shape might change how the look translates.

How to Keep Your Blonde Healthy All Summer Long

Blonde hair requires maintenance — and summer is the most demanding season. Between the pool, the sun, and the humidity, your blonde needs extra care to stay cool, healthy, and dimensional. Here's your summer blonde maintenance guide.

The Summer Threats to Your Blonde (And What to Do About Them)

Three things attack your blonde in summer:

Brassiness. UV rays and heat oxidize the color in your hair, turning cool blonde tones warm and yellow. The fix is a toner gloss — it neutralizes the warm tones and resets your color without a full appointment.

Dryness. Pool chlorine and salt water strip moisture from your hair, leaving it brittle and dull. A Davines nourishing treatment restores hydration and keeps your blonde looking fresh.

Damage. Bleach + sun = a double hit to your hair's strength. The sun's UV rays break down the protein bonds in your hair, making it weaker and more prone to breakage. K-18 bond repair rebuilds those bonds from the inside out.

The In-Salon Treatments That Protect Your Blonde

Here's what we recommend for summer blonde care:

K-18 bond repair — included in every color service at Beauty and the Beach Salon. It protects your hair during the lightening process and keeps it healthy between appointments.

Gloss/toner treatments — the mid-summer refresh you didn't know you needed. It takes less time than a full color service and resets your tone for another 4–6 weeks.

Full color or balayage — every 6–8 weeks for your main appointment. Our blonding specialists are known for keeping blondes cool, dimensional, and healthy.

At-Home Blonde Care: What Actually Works

Purple shampoo. Use it once a week, leave it on for 2–3 minutes. Any longer can tint your hair violet. Any less often and it won't do enough.

Heat protection. Every time you use a hot tool, you're opening the cuticle and letting brassiness creep in. A heat protectant keeps your color safe and your hair healthy.

Leave-in conditioner with UV protection. Yes, your hair can get sunburned too. A UV protectant spray prevents color fading and keeps your blonde from drying out.

Book your next appointment on time. Six to eight weeks is the sweet spot. Don't push past eight weeks — the longer you wait, the more brassiness sets in and the harder it is to correct.

Your Summer Blonde Plan

Here's a simple schedule to keep your blonde healthy all summer:

  • Now: Book a toner gloss if you're seeing warmth
  • Every 6–8 weeks: Full color or highlights with K-18
  • Every 3 months: K-18 treatment
  • Weekly: Purple shampoo at home
  • Daily: Heat protection + UV protectant

Your blonde doesn't have to suffer through summer. With the right routine and the right products, you can keep it cool, healthy, and dimensional all season long.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I get a toner?

Every 4–6 weeks between color appointments. If you notice your blonde looking warmer than usual, it's time for a toner gloss.

Is it bad to use purple shampoo every day?

Yes — daily use can over-tone your hair and leave a violet tint. Once a week for 2–3 minutes is the sweet spot for most blondes.

Can I swim with blonde hair?

Yes, but protect it first. Wet your hair with clean water and apply a leave-in conditioner before swimming. This reduces how much chlorine or salt water your hair absorbs.

Will K-18 help if my hair is already damaged?

Yes. K-18 is designed to repair existing damage by rebuilding broken peptide bonds. Most clients see visible improvement after one treatment, with best results after 3–4 treatments.

End of Summer Hair Refresh: Your August-Ready Guide

Summer is winding down, and your hair is telling the story. The blonde is warmer than it was in June. The ends feel drier. The frizz is still here. An end-of-summer refresh sets you up for a strong fall.

What Your Hair Needs After Summer (And Why Now Is the Time)

Your hair has been through three months of Florida summer. UV rays, pool chlorine, salt water, humidity, heat styling — it all adds up. The cuticle takes a beating, moisture escapes, and your hair's internal structure weakens.

Here's why you shouldn't wait until September: the longer damage sits, the harder it is to reverse. Acting now means an easier recovery and stronger hair going into fall. Your August schedule fills up fast — book your refresh while appointments are open.

The End-of-Summer Treatment Lineup

Depending on what your hair needs, here are your best options:

K-18 bond repair — for damaged or color-treated hair that's been through summer's worst. K-18 rebuilds broken bonds inside the hair shaft, restoring strength and elasticity. Most clients see a visible difference after one treatment.

NKST keratin smoothing treatment — for frizz that won't quit. A keratin treatment seals the cuticle, blocking humidity and keeping your hair smooth even as the weather starts to shift. Fall's cooler air can still be humid, and your hair deserves a break.

Davines nourishing treatment — for dry, brittle hair that needs moisture restored. It's a deep hydration session with professional products that leave your hair soft, shiny, and manageable.

Not sure which one you need? That's what the consultation is for. We'll look at your hair, assess the summer damage, and recommend the right treatment.

Blonde Refresh: Reset Your Tone for Fall

Fall is the perfect time to switch your blonde. Summer brights give way to warmer, more dimensional autumn tones. Your colorist can help you choose a shade that bridges the seasons — still fresh, but softer.

A toner gloss is the quickest way to reset. It neutralizes any brassiness from summer sun and preps your blonde for a new seasonal tone. Full color or balayage clients should book their regular 6–8 week refresh now.

Your August Hair Plan

Here's what to book based on your hair type:

  • Blonde & Color Loyalists: Toner gloss + trim. Book before the August rush.
  • Treatment Seekers: K-18 or keratin consult. Your hair has earned a reset.
  • Fresh Cut: Book a cut and style to remove summer damage and start fall with a clean slate.
  • Trust-First New Clients: Book a consultation. Fall is a great time to find a stylist you love.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get K-18 if I'm planning to color my hair?

Yes. In fact, we include K-18 in every color service. It protects your hair during the coloring process and leaves it stronger after.

Is it too late to do a keratin treatment if summer is almost over?

Not at all. A keratin treatment lasts 3–5 months, so getting one now means you're covered through fall and into winter. Plus, the hair you save now is hair you don't have to repair later.

What if I just want a trim?

That's fine too. A fresh cut removes split ends and makes your hair look healthier instantly. Our precision stylists are favorites for this.

My hair feels fine. Do I still need a refresh?

If your hair looks and feels healthy, you might just need a toner and a trim. But it's worth a quick consultation — sometimes damage isn't visible until a professional looks at it.
